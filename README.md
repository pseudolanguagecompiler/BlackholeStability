# HairyKnobTheory v1.0

## Black-hole thermodynamics
dM = T dS + \Omega dJ + \Phi dQ

F = M - T S - \Omega J - \Phi Q

## Superradiance condition
\omega < m \Omega_H

## Rotational energy
E_rot = M - M_{\mathrm{irr}}

For a Kerr black hole,
M_{\mathrm{irr}}^2 = \frac{1}{2}\left(M^2 + \sqrt{M^4 - J^2}\right)

## Hair and perturbations
A scalar field can form a bound cloud around a rotating black hole if the mode grows by superradiance and backreacts on the geometry.

The evolution depends on the full perturbation equations, not just on the sign of a single second derivative.

## Stability
Growing modes indicate an instability.

Stable end states, if they exist, depend on the field content, boundary conditions, and nonlinear dynamics.

## Scaling
The growth rate can depend on the black-hole mass, and the detailed timescale is model-dependent.

The total extractable rotational energy is finite and is limited by the black hole’s spin.

## Possible observables
Gravitational waves from long-lived boson clouds

Changes to the black-hole shadow

Timing or spectral anomalies in accretion systems
